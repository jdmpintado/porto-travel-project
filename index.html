Why Porto?

The Bridge Between Past and Present

Porto is one of Europe's oldest cities, with a history spanning over 2,000 years. Its UNESCO World Heritage historic center is a testament to this deep-rooted past. At the same time, Porto is a vibrant, modern city with a thriving arts scene, innovative architecture, and a forward-looking spirit.

The city is defined by the Douro River, and its iconic bridges are a central part of the cityscape. The most famous, the Dom Luís I Bridge, physically connects the historic center of Porto with Vila Nova de Gaia.

Cafés

Most famous cafés of Porto

Majestic Café

This is arguably the most famous café in Porto and one of the most beautiful in the world. It's a stunning example of Art Nouveau architecture with a glamorous, Belle Époque atmosphere, also famous for its connection to J.K. Rowling, who is said to have written some of her early Harry Potter drafts there while living in the city.

Address

R. de Santa Catarina 112, 4000-442 Porto

Café Guarany

Situated on the grand Avenida dos Aliados, this historic café opened in 1933 and has long been a meeting place for Porto's intellectuals and elite. Its interior features beautiful Art Deco design, with striking murals and a rich, traditional ambiance. It's a great spot to enjoy a coffee and soak in the city's history.

Address

Av. dos Aliados 85 89, 4000-066 Porto

Learn more

Confeitaria do Bolhão

This iconic bakery and café is a local institution. Located opposite the famous Bolhão Market, it's known for its incredible variety of traditional Portuguese pastries and cakes. It's a beloved spot that offers a more authentic, bustling Porto experience with a focus on delicious treats.

Address

R. Formosa 339, 4000-252 Porto
