Why Porto?

The Bridge Between Past and Present

Porto is one of Europe's oldest cities, with a history spanning over 2,000 years. Its UNESCO World Heritage historic center is a testament to this deep-rooted past. At the same time, Porto is a vibrant, modern city with a thriving arts scene, innovative architecture, and a forward-looking spirit.

The city is defined by the Douro River, and its iconic bridges are a central part of the cityscape. The most famous, the Dom Luís I Bridge, physically connects the historic center of Porto with Vila Nova de Gaia.

Famous cafés in Porto

Majestic Café

This is arguably the most famous café in Porto and one of the most beautiful in the world. It's a stunning example of Art Nouveau architecture with a glamorous, Belle Époque atmosphere, also famous for its connection to J.K. Rowling, who is said to have written some of her early Harry Potter drafts there while living in the city.

Café Guarany

Situated on the grand Avenida dos Aliados, this historic café opened in 1933 and has long been a meeting place for Porto's intellectuals and elite. Its interior features beautiful Art Deco design, with striking murals and a rich, traditional ambiance. It's a great spot to enjoy a coffee and soak in the city's history.

Address

Av. dos Aliados 85 89, 4000-066 Porto

Confeitaria do Bolhão

This iconic bakery and café is a local institution. Located opposite the famous Bolhão Market, it's known for its incredible variety of traditional Portuguese pastries and cakes. It's a beloved spot that offers a more authentic, bustling Porto experience with a focus on delicious treats.

Discover Porto in just 3 days

With just three days, you can get a fantastic taste of Porto's vibrant heart and its beautiful surroundings. Here is a suggested itinerary to help you make the most of your time, blending history, culture, and relaxation.


Day 1: The Historic Heart of Porto

Focus on the main city center, a UNESCO World Heritage site, by exploring its stunning landmarks on foot.

Morning:

A tiled masterpiece

Start at the São Bento Train Station, a work of art in itself. The interior walls are covered in over 20,000 "azulejos" (blue and white tiles) that depict scenes of Portuguese history. From there, take a short walk to the Porto Cathedral (Sé do Porto), a fortress-like cathedral offering a blend of Romanesque, Gothic, and Baroque architecture.

Afternoon:

Riverside charm and old-world elegance

Descend through the labyrinthine alleys of the historic Sé district to reach the Ribeira district and its lively riverfront. This area is perfect for a leisurely lunch with a view of the Douro River. Afterward, visit the ornate Church of São Francisco with its stunningly gilded interior and the adjacent Palácio da Bolsa (Stock Exchange Palace) to admire its famous Arabian Hall.

Evening:

A climb and a view

Climb the Clérigos Tower for one of the best panoramic views of the city. As the sun begins to set, head to the top level of the Dom Luís I Bridge. This offers breathtaking views of the city on one side and the port wine lodges on the other. Enjoy dinner in the Ribeira district, where the atmosphere is electric at night.


Day 2: Port Wine and Riverside Relaxation

Cross the river to Vila Nova de Gaia and experience the city's most famous tradition.

Morning:

Port wine cellars and a scenic stroll

Cross the Dom Luís I Bridge on its upper level to get to Vila Nova de Gaia. The riverfront is lined with the famous port wine cellars, known as "caves." You can take a guided tour and tasting at one of the many lodges, such as Taylor's, Graham's, or Sandeman.

Afternoon:

Douro River Cruise

After your tasting, take a relaxing "Six Bridges Cruise" on a traditional "rabelo" boat. This short cruise provides a unique perspective of the city's bridges and riverbanks. You'll see the iconic Dom Luís I Bridge from below, along with the other five bridges that span the river.

Evening:

Sunset and local cuisine

Find a perfect spot for sunset. The gardens of the Mosteiro da Serra do Pilar or the Jardim do Morro in Gaia offer incredible views back towards Porto. For dinner, try some local specialties like a "francesinha," Porto's famous and decadent sandwich.


Day 3: A Day Trip to a Cultural Gem

For your final day, venture beyond Porto to explore the rich history of the Minho region. The twin cities of Guimarães and Braga are a perfect combination and easily accessible by a short train ride.

Option 1:

Guimarães & Braga

 Guimarães

Often called the "birthplace of Portugal," this UNESCO World Heritage city is full of medieval charm. Wander through the historic center, visit the Guimarães Castle where the first King of Portugal was born, and explore the Palace of the Dukes of Braganza.

Braga

Portugal's religious capital is home to magnificent churches and sanctuaries. The main attraction is the Bom Jesus do Monte sanctuary, famous for its stunning Baroque stairway.

Option 2:

Douro Valley

For a more focused wine experience and a stunning change of scenery, take a day trip to the Douro Valley. You can book an organized tour that includes transportation, visits to "quintas" (wine estates) for tastings, and a scenic river cruise, all without the hassle of driving. The terraced vineyards cascading down to the river are a spectacular sight.

This itinerary balances key sights, local experiences, and a chance to see more of northern Portugal, ensuring you get the most out of your three days.
